Welcome & Introduction

Welcome to our github highlighting the work of LIS professionals, specifically in the Library Studies specialization, focusing on library/organization management. This project was conducted by Anna Freidman, Diana Naughton, Emma Hughes, and Sam Garcia for IS 270: Systems and Infrastructures. In order to gain insight into the field and assess how AI is impacting Library and Information Science (LIS) work, we conducted an interview with Lauren Trujillo and conducted secondary research highlighting the profession. The following is a compiled collection and analysis of our findings.

Interview

              

Lauren Trujillo serves as the Executive Director of the Santa Barbara Public Library Foundation, where she has now been for 7 years. She previously worked solely in fundraising for the public library in the form of capital and campaigns as Foundation Director. She later transitioned to the role of Executive Director where she "wears all the hats" including fundraising, marketing, board liason, tech and IT, AI consultant, etc. Here department has only 3 staff the works to raise anywehre from $1 million to $6 million each year.

Professional Context

While there aren't many specialized professional associations for this specialization in particular, there are a multitude of general associations worth looking into including:

  • American Library Association (ALA)
  • California Library Association (CLA)
  • Association of Fundraising Professionals (AFP)

If you are interested in pursuing a job in library development, library fundraising, or anything related to library foundations, conferences could be a great place to meet people and potentially hear about open positions. Some conferences professionals in this specialization might attend include:

  • American Library Association Conference
  • AFP ICON
